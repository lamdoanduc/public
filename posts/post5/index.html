Comparing two populations using hypothesis testing

March 10, 2021

Problem 1 (proportions)

  • A study shows the effect of a new drug on the treatment of a disease in two groups of patients: the first group received the drug and the other received placebo (no drug). The table below is the results:
n_patients n_cured proportion
new drug 250 150 0.60
placebo 245 118 0.48

  • We want to test if the new drug has a positive effect on the treatment or the result just happens by chance.

  • Approach: we will use the hypothesis testing (z-test)

Step 1:

  • Define the hypotheses:

    • H_0: p1 = p2, the two proportions are the same and the new drug doesn’t have any effect on the treatment.
    • H_a: p1 # p2, the new drug has positive effect on the treatment

  • Significance level:

    • We want to be 95% confidence, or 5% of Significance level (alpha = 0.05)
# Sample sizes:
n1 = 250
n2 = 245
# sampling proportion
p1 = 0.60 # new drug
p2 = 0.48 # placebo

Step 2:

Identify test statistic: Z-statistic $$z_s = \frac{(p1 - p2) - (p1_0 - p2_0)}{\sqrt{\frac{p1(1-p1)}{n1} + \frac{p2(1-p2)}{n2}}}$$

where p1_0 and p2_0 is the true population proportions. Assume there is no difference between the populations, p1_0 - p2_0 = 0.

z_s = (p1 - p2) / ((p1*(1-p1)/n1 + p2*(1-p2)/n2)**(1/2))

# round the number
z_s = round(z_s,2)
z_s

2.7

Step 3:

Determine the p-value

from scipy.stats import norm
p_value = 1 - norm.cdf(abs(z_s))
p_value = round(p_value,4)
p_value

0.0035

Step 4: Comparing p-value and the significance level

Because the p_value is smaller than the significance level, we can reject the null hypothesis. It supports the alternative hypothesis that the new drug has an effect on the treatment.

Problem 2 (Means)

  • A new pedagogical method is applied in teaching for group A of students in a school. Another group, group B, of students was taught with the ordinary pedagogical method. The same test was given to the two groups, and the results (grades) are shown in the below table:
n_students mean_grades standard deviation
group A 200 15.4 1.8
group B 245 14.1 2.2
  • If the new teaching method is more effective than the ordinary method or the test results happens by chance?
  • Approach: we will use hypothesis testing (z-test).

Step 1:

  • Define the hypotheses:

    • H_0: The two methods are equally effective.
    • H_a: There new method is more effective than the ordinary one.

  • Significance level:

    • We want to be 99.9% confidence, so 0.1% of Significance level (alpha = 0.001)
# Sample size:
n1, n2 = 200, 245
# sample means
x1_b  = 15.4
x2_b  = 14.9
# sample standard deviation
sigma1 = 1.8
sigma2 = 1.9

Step 2: Identify test statistic: Z-statistic

  • Since we don’t have information about the populations, we have to use the sample standard deviations: $$z_s = \frac{(\overline{x_1} - \overline{x_2}) - (\mu_1 - \mu_2)}{\sqrt{\frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1} + \frac{\sigma_1^2}{n_1}}}$$

where \mu_1 and \mu_2 is the true population means. Assume there is no difference between the populations, p1_0 - p2_0 = 0.

z_s = (x1_b - x2_b) / ((sigma1**2)/(n1) + (sigma2**2)/(n2)) **(1/2)

# round the number
z_s = round(z_s,2)
z_s

2.84

Step 3:

Determine the p-value

p_value = 1 - norm.cdf(z_s)
p_value = round(p_value,6)
p_value

0.002256

Step 4: Comparing p-value and the significance level

Because the p_value is larger than the significance level (alpha = 0.001), we can not reject the null hypothesis. It also means the data does not support the alternative hypothesis that the new method is more effective than the ordinary one is.
